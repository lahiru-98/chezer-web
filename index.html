Introduction

Advancement of human computer interactions have led to the development of automated systems for the study of facial emotions. Currently, human intervention is required to identify the emotional state of an individual, thus our study proposes an automated process using a novel mobile application named Chezer which aims to identify the emotion expression and recognition abilities of children. The main objective of this study is to provide an affordable application to low-income families by eliminating the need for them to visit clinics for child emotion related concerns. Chezer uses a multi-sensory gaming approach in developing games to identify the emotion expression and recognition ability of a child, where a level and a scenario-based gaming methods are implemented respectively. The games include various methods such as visual stimulation, audio stimulation and quiz-based assessments to aid a parent in analyzing the ability of their children. To evaluate the games, an emotion prediction model based on CNN which yields an accuracy of 90% is incorporated along with other models to detect valence, arousal, and emotion levels. Valence and arousal detection models yield RMSE scores of 0.23 and 0.05 respectively whereas the emotion level model which is a Random Forest classifier yields 86.5% accuracy. Incorporation of valence and arousal along with an age wise emotion level prediction makes the study unique in the context of children. LIRIS and EmoReact children video datasets were used for the model training and testing purposes. Overall, combination of all these features in a single application makes the study novel.

Literature Review

A Literature survey was conducted to compare the existing solutions with the proposed solution. Paper [1] is a study done on music recommendation according to contexts such as demographics and contextual information such as weather and holidays to recommend music. There they have implemented two steps in the recommendation process, the first step for retrieving the similar contexts and the second step for retrieving similar users. As a result, they have obtained 0.54 average precision with an 8% increase from the previous research. In conclusion, they have proved that demographics can improve recommendation performance. Paper [2] is a research conducted on convolutional neural networks that can identify the age and gender from facial images, which they have focused on proposing a neural network that can be adequate for the limited number of data and fit for the real-world images that are not shot in a lab setting. There the research has achieved 86.8% of gender classification and 84.7% on age estimation. The neural network used here in this research can help to train the deep learning model. In the aspect of emotion-based music recommendation, Anurag De & Ashim Saha have studied various approaches available for automatic real-time human emotion recognition based on facial expression detection [3]. In summary, they have studied various approaches to real-time emotion recognition through the detection of facial expression from a live video feed and have shown positive results on that. And another group of researchers has studied Emotion and activity-based music player using machine learning called in the paper [4]. In the aspect of voice-based emotion recognition, the paper [5] illustrates how the researchers have created a model to identify the emotion and demographics (age/gender) by the voice using mandarin utterances collected that have various emotion classes. Finally, they have compared the utterance level DNN approach with the DNN-ELM approach and acquired better-weighted accuracy on both the age/gender and emotion recognition task. Another group of researchers has done a study on the feature fusion method based on an extreme learning machine for speech emotion recognition [6]. Here the users are proposing a method to address issues in the state-of-the-art model CNN-BLSTM, convolutional neural network (CNN) that directly extracts features on spectrograms, and bidirectional long, short-term memory (BLSTM)CNN-BSTM model, which is used for speech emotion recognition. In the process, they have combined some different methodologies and compared them with each other to identify the best technology to understand emotion in speech. And as the final solution, they have proposed a feature fusion method that combines Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)-based features and heuristic-based discriminative features. In the aspect of gesture-based controlling of the mobile app, a study which was done on the paper [7] is based on a hand gesture recognition algorithm implemented using K-NN for video annotation purposes. The researchers were primarily interested in identifying 20 hand gestures. They processed the data using a variety of techniques and methodologies and obtained results. Finally, the researchers find that KNN outperforms principal component analysis (PCA), the subtraction technique, and the HGR method. Another research was conducted on dynamic hand gesture recognition from deep learning technologies. [8] It used a dataset of 2800 samples with 14 different hand gesture types. They used hand skeletal sequence for dynamic hand gesture recognition; handshape descriptor called the shape of connected joints for hand detection. From the literature review we have done, a conclusion can be made that the research is viable.

Research Gap

There are various research carried out in the field of Human emotion analysis as it is useful in the prior identification of any psychological disorders within human, yet there is no realization that the emotional cues expressed imply different aspects under varying age. Therefore, a common misinterpretation observed is that the assumption of emotional cues expressed by adults and children to be much more similar irrespective to their varying age groups. Studies also reveal that the facial expressivity increases with age and most importantly children continue to learn how to produce facial expressions even in late childhood. Even though literatures reveal that age is a dominating factor in understanding facial emotions accurately, yet researchers have not come up with accurate findings on the influence of age in child emotion with justifying facts. In fact, the analysis on the different aspects of emotion variation in children could be much more challenging than that of adults due to the changes in facial features and emotional cues during their growth and development. Previous work on such aspect had only addressed the emotional states of adults dimensionally whereas modelling of the variability of temporal facial expression factors are neglected. Valence and Arousal are two significant dimensions of emotion that can be used to analyze rich facial emotions. Analyzing the emotion alone does not cover the entire range of emotions which hinders the analysis of practical systems. The levels of arousal and valence for a type of emotion or the comparisons between the two for an emotion expressed by children has not been considered in previous works. Moreover, previous works either consider the analysis of emotion variations or the detection of arousal and valence levels as separate works. While there exist few works on adult facial emotion level recognition, researchers have been mainly focusing on assigning a nominal / regression value indicating the intensity of the emotion which is far from the perception of human towards emotion level estimation. The facial expressions are broken down into individual components called Action units (AU) presented by FACS. The analysis of facial expressions using AU is one of very few techniques available in assessing emotions in real time. Yet the prediction of ordinal emotion levels by identifying the patterns of AU combinations with Age for growing children is an unseen scope of emotion analysis When the emotion expression ability of the child is considered, previous works focus mainly on identifying the ability of child to express the given emotion only. Factors like the gender, age and inborn emotion of child respective to several environments were not considered. More importantly these research are conducted under a controlled environment. Research based on identifying the ability of a child to express an emotion considering a gaming approach do exist, but such applications native to Sri Lanka are yet not available. Existing games also require the child to upload a recorded video of them which could hinder the privacy of the child Analyzing the ability of a child to recognize an emotion has been focused on some research, yet the overlapping of emotions has not been taken into consideration. Further, therapeutic approach / intervention to children who have difficulty or weakness in recognizing facial emotions has not been implemented so far. Even though the emotion recognition ability/ disability of a child is considered, the percentage of the recognition ability/disability is not considered

Research Problem

It is clear that emotion analysis is required to understand the mental growth status of children. Children undergo a significant mental growth throughout childhood which causes variations of emotion from time to time. Moreover, they do not have the ability to stimulate muscles, recognize emotions and express emotions more precisely. Thus, a need arises for an automated method to analyze the status of emotion in child which we enable through a mobile application. When it comes to rich facial emotions; understanding the variation of emotion levels, valence and arousal, emotion expression and recognition abilities of children are not available as a single implementation which can aid child screening with ease in their natural environment. No previous solutions are available to analyze the emotion levels considering the action unit activation. Rich dimensions such as valence and arousal are not yet analyzed in terms of children. No gaming mobile applications are available that could assess such aspects along with emotion expression and recognition abilities in children so far.

Research Objectives

To provide a system that could analyze the rich facial emotion of Children to keep track of the mental and cognitive development of the Child.

  1. Identification of the overall Emotion level variation in Child video sources considering their Age variations.
  2. Develop a Gaming mobile application which can identify children who have difficulties in expressing facial emotions considering their Age variations
  3. Identification of the change of emotion expressed by Child in video sources along with the Valence and Arousal levels
  4. Develop a narration-based Gaming mobile application which can identify children who have difficulties in recognizing facial emotions and provides an intervention through the game considering the age variation

Methodology

The proposed system to monitor the emotion expression and recognition abilities in children is developed on the basis of a mobile application comprising of four components to address the research objectives. The front-end of the mobile application is developed using React Native and models are deployed to Google Cloud Platform and loaded using Google Cloud Functions for predictions. Firebase is considered as the primary database and google sheets are used to collect data while a child plays the game with the aid of a micro-service developed using Heroku Platform. All required models and details are loaded to perform an analysis that is made available to the user through the mobile interface. Application incorporates models for emotion prediction, valence arousal prediction and emotion level annotation, trained and tested using LIRIS and Emo-React video datasets. Emotion prediction model is developed based on a deep learning Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model while the models for valence and arousal are developed based on deep learning Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) model. Frames from datasets were extracted, preprocessed and fed to the models for training purpose. OpenCV was used for face detection. In addition, Adam optimizers, ReLu and Sigmoid activation functions are incorporated. Emotion Level prediction model is developed based on Machine learning algorithm, where emotion level annotations are conducted by novel techniques such as Explanatory Factor Analysis(EFA), and Independent Sample t-test. Action Unit (AU) features are extracted, preprocessed and fed to the model for training purpose. Open Face toolkit is used for the AU analysis. Gaming approach has been implemented by incorporating three levels to the game. In FEE, the first level analyzes the ability of a child to express the emotions by analyzing the ability to imitate their emotion as shown by an emoji based expression. Children are allowed to imitate the same emojis in second level but without the camera mirror, so that they express the emotion spontaneously without adjustments. Third level is designed with the voice recognition module, where the child expresses the emotion that is requested by the voice output. The emotion expressed during the game is captured through the phone camera and provided as inputs to the CNN model, where the prediction of the actual emotion expressed by the child is obtained and validated against the expected emotion. FER task has a scenario based gaming approach where the scenarios are designed in a way that the children are driven to be empathetic about it by using well known cartoon characters. The first level includes scenarios with a single character, where at the end of each scenario the child has to identify emotions expressed by the characters. The second level attempts in analyzing the emotion recognition ability of a child by displaying short videos of unknown people. Here, the child is assessed by their ability to recognize emotions in visual representation of strangers. In the third level of the game, child is shown two characters that displays two different emotions. Then a quiz is used to assess the ability of child, where the child has to distinguish between the characters that displayed the requested emotion.
